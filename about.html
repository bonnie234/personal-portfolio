A Bit About Me

Growing up in rural Wisconsin, my childhood days were filled with projects and adventures. Many hours were spent with animals or in the garden. But when indoors, I was sitting at my desk drawing and writing. It was sheer determination to which I tried to draw a horse in perfect proportion. Often I would come up with story and illustrate the pictures to go with it.

I wasn’t sure of my major when I started college. However, one of my favorite things to do was to reconstruct my old clothes into new clothes. It was opportunity to improve upon a design, once the previous no longer fit my needs. This hobbie propelled me to the textile design program. I followed the program from the University of Wisconsin to the Fashion Institute of Design in New York City and from there started my 9 year career developing fabric color and patterns for women’s apparel.

In NYC, I gravitated towards the many art experiences the city has to offer. Exhibits, curated stores, musical performances and travel allowed me to understand how others artistically organize information and create a story or mood, as well as overall trends. I continued this exploration online and found many websites and social media sites which conveyed the brand identities. This lead me to taking continuing education courses in online marketing and branding.

But is was discovering user experience design brought many of these things together. Learning UX/UI design and relocating to Chicago has been a great opportunity to expand my skills and thought process around development. I continue to examine successful interactions and strengthen knowledge of web applications.
